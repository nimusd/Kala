Saxophone Documentation

Overview

The Saxophone container is a digital waveguide physical model of a conical-bore reed instrument, based on the Saxofony algorithm developed by Perry R. Cook and Gary P. Scavone in the Synthesis ToolKit (STK) and extended with the nonlinear waveguide technique by Romain Michon. It models the interaction between a reed and a conical air column: a ReedTable nonlinearity drives two coupled delay lines split at the blow position, filtered through a OneZero loss filter, producing a tone that blends the character of soprano saxophone, clarinet, and oboe depending on the parameter settings.

Because the model is self-oscillating, it produces a continuous pitched tone for the full duration of any note. The pitch is tracked continuously, so pitch curves and portamento in the Score Canvas work without artifacts. Legato transitions preserve the bore's vibrating state, allowing smooth slurs between notes. The optional nonlinear allpass section (Michon extension) adds harmonic richness inside the waveguide feedback loop, pushing the timbre toward richer, brasier, or more complex territory.

How It Works

The algorithm is a split digital waveguide simulation of a conical-bore reed instrument (Cook & Scavone, STK Saxofony). The bore is modelled as two delay lines split at the blow position; the reed is modelled by a clamped linear function (ReedTable); a OneZero filter models high-frequency losses at the open end. Each sample is computed as follows:

breathP      = breathPressure × envelope
             + noiseGain × noise()
             + vibratoGain × 0.1 × sin(vibratoPhase)

d0           = delay0.lastOut        (open-end segment)
d1           = delay1.lastOut        (reed-end segment)

[NL allpass applied to d0 if nlAmount > 0]

temp         = −0.95 × oneZeroFilter( d0 )   (open-end reflection)
output       = temp − d1                      (pressure at blow point)
pressureDiff = breathP − output
reedCoeff    = reedTable( pressureDiff )

delay1.tick( temp )
delay0.tick( breathP − pressureDiff × reedCoeff − temp )

return output × 0.3

The key components are:

  • Delay 0 (open-end segment): A linearly-interpolating delay line modelling the longer bore section from the blow position to the open bell end. Its length is proportional to (1 − blowPosition) × totalDelay.
  • Delay 1 (reed-end segment): A shorter delay line modelling the bore from the blow position back to the reed. Length is blowPosition × totalDelay, where totalDelay = sampleRate / pitch − 1.5 (the 1.5 compensates for the OneZero filter's phase delay).
  • ReedTable: A clamped linear function modelling the reed valve: clamp(offset + slope × pressureDiff, −1, 1), where offset = 0.4 + 0.6 × reedAperture and slope = 0.1 + 0.4 × reedStiffness. This approximation of Smith's 1986 clarinet reed model is the primary nonlinear oscillation mechanism.
  • OneZero filter: A simple one-zero FIR low-pass y[n] = 0.5 × (x[n] + x[n−1]) applied to the open-end output, modelling high-frequency absorption at the bell.
  • Nonlinear allpass (Michon extension): A first-order allpass filter applied to the open-end delay output (d0) before the OneZero filter, inside the waveguide feedback loop. See Nonlinear Section below.

Breath Envelope

A simple attack/release envelope is applied to the breath pressure based on note progress:

  • Attack: 8% of note duration — ramps from 0 to full breath pressure. Simulates the onset of blowing. Skipped for legato notes.
  • Sustain: Full breath pressure.
  • Release: Last 5% of note duration — ramps back to zero. Simulates stopping the breath.

This envelope affects only the internal excitation; the overall dynamics of the note are controlled by the Score Canvas as usual.

Nonlinear Section (Michon Extension)

When nlAmount > 0, a first-order nonlinear allpass filter is applied to the open-end delay output on each sample, before the OneZero reflection filter:

H(z) = (θ + z⁻¹) / (1 + θ·z⁻¹)
y[n] = θ·(x[n] − y[n−1]) + x[n−1]

Because it is placed inside the waveguide loop (not as a post-processing effect), the allpass genuinely modifies the bore resonance — each round trip the signal passes through it, accumulating harmonic content with every reflection. The allpass coefficient θ is derived from one of five modulation sources, scaled by nlAmount and the ramp-in envelope, then clamped to (−0.999, 0.999) for stability:

Type Source Character
0 Open-end signal Direct signal-dependent modulation. Thickens tone and adds subtle harmonic richness that tracks the bore vibration. The most natural-sounding type for saxophone-like timbres.
1 Leaky average of open-end signal Slow, DC-like drift of the allpass coefficient. Produces a gentle tonal shift over time rather than per-sample modulation.
2 Open-end signal² Always positive; responds to energy bursts in the bore. Asymmetric modulation that emphasises the attack and forte moments.
3 Sine wave at nlFreqMod Hz Periodic, independent tremolo-like modulation. At low nlFreqMod values produces slow tonal wobble; at higher values produces audible sidebands.
4 Sine wave at pitch Hz Sine locked to the fundamental frequency. Produces harmonically coherent waveshaping that tracks pitch — consistent timbre across the register.

The nlAttack parameter ramps the effect from zero to full depth over the specified number of seconds at the start of each note. This prevents a hard click when the nonlinearity switches on and allows the bore tone to establish itself before the effect is applied.

Tip: Start with nlType = 0 at low nlAmount (0.05–0.15) for the most musical, natural-sounding extension — it adds upper harmonics that give the model more of the characteristic "cutting" quality of a real saxophone. Types 3 and 4 at higher amounts produce more overtly electronic or FM-like timbres.

Parameter Guide

Breath Pressure

Controls the overall driving force of the simulated breath and is the primary volume control.

  • Below ~0.55: Insufficient to sustain oscillation at most reed settings — the model underblows
  • 0.60–0.75: Lighter, more clarinet-like tone — stable with a prominent fundamental
  • 0.75–0.85: Full-bodied tone with good harmonic content (default 0.70)
  • Above 0.90: Risk of instability or overblowing, especially at high pitches

Reed Stiffness

Controls the slope of the ReedTable function, determining how sharply the reed valve transitions between open and closed states.

  • 0.2–0.4: Soft reed — gradual transition, rounder tone with a strong fundamental
  • 0.4–0.6: Balanced reed character (default 0.5)
  • 0.7–0.9: Stiff reed — abrupt valve action, brighter and more nasal tone with more upper harmonics

Reed Aperture

Sets the resting opening of the reed — the offset in the ReedTable function. This determines how much of the breath pressure range is "seen" by the reed as open vs. closed.

  • 0.2–0.4: Nearly closed at rest — requires higher breath pressure to open; produces a more focused, darker tone
  • 0.5–0.6: Moderate opening (default 0.5)
  • 0.7–0.9: Wide opening — the reed is biased open; softer articulation and a more hollow quality

Blow Position

Determines the split point of the two bore delay lines. This is the most distinctive parameter of the Saxofony model — it sets which part of the bore each delay line represents.

  • 0.05–0.15: Near the reed end — the shorter reed segment dominates; bright, clarinet-like tone with strong odd harmonics
  • 0.20–0.30: Balanced split; fuller harmonic content more reminiscent of soprano saxophone (default 0.20)
  • 0.40–0.60: Near-equal split; produces a rounder, more oboe-like tone
  • Above 0.6: Increasingly unstable — the open-end segment becomes very short

Noise Gain

Adds breath turbulence noise additively to the breath pressure, simulating the aerodynamic noise of real wind playing.

  • 0.0: Pure, clean tone
  • 0.05–0.15: Subtle breathiness — natural reed instrument character (default 0.20)
  • 0.25–0.40: Noticeably breathy, airy sound; useful for sul fiato effects

Vibrato

Vibrato is disabled by default (vibratoGain = 0.0). When enabled, a sine wave modulates the breath pressure at vibratoFreq Hz with a depth proportional to vibratoGain.

  • Musical vibrato: vibratoFreq = 4.5–6.5 Hz, vibratoGain = 0.05–0.15
  • Jazz-style wide vibrato: vibratoFreq = 4.0–5.0 Hz, vibratoGain = 0.15–0.25
  • vibratoFreq has no effect when vibratoGain = 0.0

NL Amount

The primary nonlinearity control. At 0.0 the nonlinear allpass is completely bypassed. As the value increases, the allpass coefficient modulation grows and the tone becomes progressively richer in upper partials.

  • 0.0: Pure STK Saxofony — no nonlinearity
  • 0.05–0.15: Subtle harmonic enrichment — adds the "cut" and brightness associated with real saxophones
  • 0.20–0.40: Clearly audible tonal thickening and increased upper harmonic content
  • 0.5–0.8: Strong modulation — noticeable waveshaping and spectral spread
  • 0.9–1.0: Aggressive; experimental territory

NL Freq Mod

Only active with nlType = 3 (independent sine). For nlType = 4 the pitch frequency is used automatically and this parameter is ignored.

  • 0.1–5 Hz: Very slow sweep — almost static tonal drift
  • 5–15 Hz: Tremolo-rate modulation; audible as a rhythmic tonal pulse
  • 30–80 Hz: Audio-rate sideband generation
  • 80–200 Hz: Rich FM-like sidebands at high nlAmount

NL Attack

Ramps the nonlinearity from 0 to full depth at note start. A short attack (0.01–0.05 s) lets the effect appear almost immediately. A long attack (0.5–2.0 s) gradually introduces the nonlinearity as the note progresses.

Legato Behavior

For legato note transitions the bore delay buffers are preserved. The oscillation continues uninterrupted from the previous note; only the delay line lengths are updated to the new pitch. The breath attack ramp is skipped so there is no re-onset transient between slurred notes. The nonlinear allpass state (filter memory and oscillator phase) is also preserved.

For non-legato notes, all delay lines, the OneZero filter state, and the nonlinear allpass state are fully cleared.

Tail Behavior

Like other breath-driven models, the saxophone tone decays rapidly once blowing stops. In tail mode the breath pressure envelope ramps to zero and the bore delay buffers drain to silence. This typically takes less than 100 ms, after which the audio engine detects silence and closes the tail cleanly. The nonlinear allpass remains active during the tail so the decay character is consistent with the sustained tone.

reedAperture:   0.40

A very low blow position makes the reed-end segment dominant, which strongly emphasises odd harmonics — giving the characteristic hollow, clarinet-like tone where even harmonics are weak.

Vibrato Expression

vibratoFreq:  5.2
vibratoGain:  0.10

Or modulate vibratoGain with an Envelope Engine to bring vibrato in gradually over the note, simulating a classical saxophone performance style.

Nonlinear Warmth (Michon Type 0)

nlType:   0
nlAmount: 0.10
nlAttack: 0.12

Signal-dependent modulation inside the waveguide loop adds harmonic richness that grows naturally with the bore's own vibration. At this level, the effect is subtle but clearly audible as additional upper partials and a slightly more complex, "alive" tone.

Harmonic Locking (Michon Type 4)

nlType:   4
nlAmount: 0.20
nlAttack: 0.10

The modulation sine is locked to the fundamental pitch, so the added harmonics track every note. Gives a consistent, richer timbre across the full pitch range.

Combining with Formant Body

[Saxophone] → signalOut → [Formant Body] → signalOut → [Output]

The Formant Body processor can add vowel-like resonances. Low f1/f2 values (300–700 Hz) reinforce the warm lower partials; higher values (800–2000 Hz) bring out the nasal quality common in real saxophone tones.

Combining with Reverb

[Saxophone] → signalOut → [IR Convolution] → signalOut → [Output]
  wetDry: 0.25–0.45

A small room or concert hall IR places the dry physical model in a realistic acoustic context and softens the somewhat clinical quality of the pure waveguide output.

Troubleshooting

No Sound or Very Faint

  1. Increase breathPressure — must be above ~0.55 to sustain oscillation with typical reed settings
  2. Check reedAperture is not too high (above 0.85 biases the reed fully open, reducing the valve action)
  3. Ensure blowPosition is not at 0 or 1 (degenerate boundary conditions)

Tone Unstable or Noisy

  1. Reduce nlAmount — high values inside the feedback loop can cause instability at borderline operating points
  2. Reduce breathPressure slightly; values near 0.90 are prone to overblowing at many pitch/reed combinations
  3. Move blowPosition closer to 0.20; extreme values shorten one delay line significantly and can cause aliasing at high pitches

Tone Too Clarinet-Like

  1. Increase blowPosition toward 0.25–0.35
  2. Reduce reedStiffness slightly
  3. Add a small nlAmount (0.08–0.15, type 0) to introduce more even harmonics

Tone Too Bright / Harsh

  1. Reduce reedStiffness toward 0.3–0.4
  2. Increase blowPosition slightly
  3. Add a Rolloff Processor or LP/HP Filter after the Saxophone container to attenuate high frequencies

Tone Too Dark / Lacks Presence

  1. Increase reedStiffness toward 0.6–0.7
  2. Reduce blowPosition slightly toward 0.12–0.18
  3. Add a small nlAmount (type 0) to enrich upper harmonics

NL Effect Too Abrupt at Note Start

  1. Increase nlAttack — a value of 0.3–0.8 s gives the nonlinearity time to fade in smoothly
  2. Reduce nlAmount — even small amounts can be surprisingly audible in the attack phase at high reed stiffness

Technical Notes

Pitch Tracking

The total bore delay is recalculated whenever the pitch changes:

totalDelay = sampleRate / pitch − 1.5

The 1.5 compensates for the combined phase delay of the OneZero filter (0.5 samples at DC) plus one sample of computational latency. The two segment lengths are updated simultaneously:

delay0 length = (1 − blowPosition) × totalDelay
delay1 length =      blowPosition  × totalDelay

Reed Table

The ReedTable is a clamped linear function approximating Smith's 1986 clarinet reed model:

offset     = 0.4 + 0.6 × reedAperture
slope      = 0.1 + 0.4 × reedStiffness
reedCoeff  = clamp(offset + slope × pressureDiff, −1, 1)

When pressureDiff is small (light blowing), the output is near offset; at high differential pressure the reed closes (coefficient approaches −1) or fully opens (coefficient approaches +1). The combination of reedAperture and reedStiffness shapes the entire pressure-to-flow transfer characteristic.

No STK Dependency

The model is implemented entirely within Kala (saxophonemodel.h / saxophonemodel.cpp) with no dependency on the STK library. All DSP components — the delay lines, OneZero filter, ReedTable, LCG noise source, vibrato oscillator, and nonlinear allpass — are self-contained.
