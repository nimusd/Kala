Kala Companion

Kala Companion is a built-in AI assistant that lives inside Kala. It has two roles:

  • Guide — explains Kala's concepts, containers, and workflow in plain language.
  • Builder — constructs sounit graphs and writes music on your behalf, step by step, inside the running application.

When the Companion builds something, it uses the same operations you would use by hand — every change lands in the undo stack, so you can always press Ctrl+Z to step back.

Opening the Companion

Press Ctrl+Shift+A, or go to View → AI Companion. The panel docks on the right side of the main window. You can drag it to the bottom or undock it entirely.

  • Type your message and press Enter to send.
  • Press Shift+Enter to insert a newline without sending.

Setting Up

Go to Settings → AI Companion. Choose a provider and fill in the required fields, then click Apply.

Ollama (local, recommended)

Runs entirely on your machine — no internet required, no API key, no data leaves your computer. Install Ollama, pull a model, and point Kala at it.

ollama pull qwen2.5-coder:14b

Settings: Provider = Ollama, URL = http://localhost:11434/v1, Model = qwen2.5-coder:14b (or whatever you pulled). API Key can be left as ollama.

Recommended models for Kala:
qwen3-coder:30b — very capable, needs ~20 GB VRAM
qwen2.5-coder:14b — good balance, needs ~10 GB VRAM
qwen2.5-coder:7b — lighter, needs ~6 GB VRAM

Cloud Providers

For more powerful responses, connect to a cloud API. You will need an API key from the provider's website. Conversations are sent to their servers.

What the Companion Can Do

Answer Questions

Ask anything about Kala — containers, connections, the score canvas, tuning systems, signal chain design. The Companion has the full synthesis model in its context.

"What's the difference between Rolloff Processor and 10-Band EQ?"
"How do I make a note ring out after I release it?"
"What does the modulate connection function do?"

Build Sounit Graphs

Describe the sound you want. The Companion creates the containers, connects them, and sets the parameters — all in the currently open track.

"Build me a breathy flute with a slow brightness swell."
"Make a bowed cello with a wood room reverb."
"Create a plucked string with a long tail using Karplus Strong."
"Load my bowed-lyric instrument and add a plate reverb to it."
"Add an envelope that swells up to full brightness and fades back by the end of the note."
"Use the Fibonacci spectrum as the harmonic content."
"Remove the connection between the Envelope Engine and the Rolloff Processor."

The Companion will confirm what it built and offer to play a preview. You can refine the result by asking follow-up questions.

Browse the Library

The Companion knows your full instrument library. Ask it what's available:

"What bowed string instruments do I have?"
"Show me all the drone sounds."
"Load the String Expressive Cantabile into this track."

It also knows your impulse responses and envelope library, and can apply them directly without you opening any inspector:

"What reverbs do I have? Apply the Nice Wood Room to this instrument."
"Load the Swell envelope into this Envelope Engine."
"Load the Fibonacci spectrum into Harmonic Generator 1."

Manage Sound Variations

The Companion can create, switch, rename and delete sounit variations on any track — the same operations available in the Sound Engine panel, but driven by conversation.

"Save the current sound as a variation called 'Wood Room'."
"Load the bowed-lyric instrument as a variation on this track named 'Lyric'."
"Switch to the Plate variation so I can tweak its reverb."
"Go back to the base sounit."
"Rename variation 2 from 'Plate' to 'Long Plate'."
"Delete the Wood Room variation."
"Apply the Lyric variation to all the long notes."

A typical workflow: build the base sound, ask the Companion to save it as a variation, then switch to it and ask for edits (add reverb, change EQ) without touching the base. Every switch is undoable.

Write Music

The Companion can place notes on the score canvas, set the scale and tempo, and organise notes across multiple tracks.

"Set the scale to Raga Yaman, base D4, tempo 72 BPM."
"Write a simple descending C major melody on track 0 over 4 bars at 90 BPM."
"Add a cello pizzicato bass line on track 1 under the melody on track 0."
"Clear all notes on track 2 and start fresh."
Tip: The Companion works with the track you currently have open in the Sounit Builder. Switch to the right track before asking it to build or modify a sound.

Edit Notes

The Companion can modify existing notes in many ways — pitch, duration, timing, and more.

"Transpose all the notes up an octave."
"Move the melody up a perfect fifth."
"Transpose the selected notes up 3 scale degrees."
"Set the duration of the long notes to 2000 ms."
"Stretch all the notes to be 1.5× longer without moving their start times."
"Duplicate the first phrase and place it immediately after, an octave higher."
"Set all notes on track 0 to exactly 440 Hz."

Pitch can be transposed by a ratio (2.0 = octave up, 1.5 = perfect fifth) or by scale degrees (walks the current scale, including octave wrapping). All edits are undoable.

Select Notes by Pitch or Track

The Companion can select notes on the canvas for you — by pitch range, by track, or based on what you already have highlighted — and then immediately act on them.

"Select all notes above 400 Hz."
"Select the notes below 200 Hz on track 1."
"Work on the notes I have selected right now."
"Select everything between 300 and 600 Hz and shift it 200 ms earlier."

Selected notes are visually highlighted on the canvas. The Companion chains selection and editing in a single request — you don't need two separate steps.

Shift Notes in Time

Move notes forward or backward in time — the whole canvas, a pitch range, or a specific selection.

"Move all the notes at around 300 Hz 300 ms earlier."
"Shift the selected notes forward by half a bar."
"Push everything on track 2 back by 500 ms."

The entire shift is one undo command — Ctrl+Z restores all moved notes at once.

Dynamics and Accentuation

The Companion can set dynamics on individual notes and apply rhythmic accent patterns across whole passages — including subtle humanization so no two beats sound exactly the same.

"Apply a 5/4 accent pattern: beat 1 strong, beat 3 medium, beat 4 light, the rest lighter."
"Add some humanization to the dynamics — keep the beat pattern but vary each note slightly."
"Make the selected notes a bit softer, but not all exactly the same."
"Draw a crescendo across the first 8 bars then fade back."

The accent pattern is time-signature-aware: if your piece has a meter change mid-way, each note is evaluated against the time signature active at its own position. All changes are undoable in one step.

Expressive Curves and Vibrato

The Companion can read and write the per-note expressive curves (dynamics, brightness, bow pressure, etc.) as well as vibrato settings.

"Show me the expressive curves on the first note."
"Draw a bell-shaped brightness curve on the selected notes."
"Set a slow vibrato on all the notes — rate 5 Hz, gentle pitch depth."
"Apply a late-entry vibrato that starts halfway through each note."
"Remove vibrato from the short notes."

Curve points use normalised time (0 = note start, 1 = note end) and normalised value (0–1). The curve shapes the parameter over the lifetime of each note. Vibrato supports pitch depth, amplitude depth, onset time, and an intensity envelope for natural swell-in.

Shape Phrases (Score Canvas Operations)

The Companion can apply all the right-click operations from the score canvas directly to notes — no manual selection required.

"Link the first three notes as legato."
"Apply SineInOut easing to even out the rhythm."
"Scale the timing by 0.75 to speed it up."
"Snap all notes to the current scale."
"Make the sustained drone notes continuous."
"Duplicate the phrase and put it right after, pitched a fifth higher."

All operations push to the undo stack, so Ctrl+Z always works.

Tempo and Time Signature Changes

The Companion can manage the full tempo map — the opening meter and any mid-piece changes.

"Set the opening tempo to 92 BPM in 6/8."
"Add a ritardando: change to 72 BPM at bar 9."
"Add a time signature change to 5/4 at bar 5."
"Show me all the tempo changes in the piece."
"Remove the tempo change at bar 9."

Mid-piece changes affect bar line display, beat dynamics, and playback timing. Changes are undoable.

Playback and Transport

The Companion can control playback directly — start, stop, seek to any position, and set up a loop region.

"Play the score."
"Stop."
"Go to bar 5 and play from there."
"Loop bars 3 to 7 so I can listen to that passage."
"Play from 4 seconds in."
"Clear the loop."

When a loop is set, playback cycles automatically between the start and end points until you stop or clear the loop. Bar and beat positions are 1-based and respect the tempo map.

Manage Tracks

The Companion can add, rename, and delete instrument tracks.

"Add a new track called 'Cello 2'."
"Rename track 0 to 'Violin I'."
"Delete track 3."
"What tracks do I have?"

After adding a track, ask the Companion to load an instrument into it immediately:

"Add a new track, call it 'Viola', and load the bowed-expressive sounit into it."

Open and Save Projects

The Companion can open and save .kala project files.

"What projects do I have?"
"Open the Souvenir de Florence project."
"Save the project."
"Save as D:/Music/kala/projects/my-piece.kala"

Sound Engine Settings

The Companion can read and update the Sound Engine Settings tab — the min/max slider ranges for every container type, and project audio settings.

"Show me the current drift engine settings."
"Widen the drift amount range so I can go higher than 0.1."
"Change the project sample rate to 48000 Hz."
"Set the PAD bandwidth ceiling to 40 cents."

Changes are saved immediately and take effect the next time you open an inspector panel.

Undo and Redo

The Companion can undo or redo its own actions programmatically — useful if it makes a mistake mid-build.

"Undo that."
"Undo the last three steps."
"Redo."

Change Your Library Location

If your Kala files are not in the default ~/Music/kala folder, just tell the Companion:

"My kala files are at D:/Music/kala"

It will remember this permanently. All instrument, project, spectrum, and IR scans will use the new location from then on.

Tips for Good Results

  • Be specific about the sound. "A warm cello" is good. "A warm cello with a slow attack and some room reverb" is better.
  • Ask it to play a preview. Say "play it" or "preview" after a build and the Companion will trigger playback in the Sounit Builder.
  • Iterate. After a build, ask "make it brighter" or "add more reverb" — it reads the current graph state before making changes.
  • Multi-step builds work. The Companion can chain up to 12 tool calls in a single response, so complex graphs (6–8 containers) are built in one go.
  • Ask about what you have. "What instruments are in my alto folder?" or "What spectra do I have?" are useful starting points.
  • Use loops for auditioning. Ask the Companion to loop a passage while you adjust parameters by hand — the loop stays active until you clear it.
  • Chain requests. "Select the notes above 400 Hz, apply a brightness swell, then loop bars 2 to 4 and play" all works in one message.

Limitations

  • String parameters (digit string DNA on Harmonic Generator) cannot be set programmatically. The Companion will guide you to the right inspector field.
  • The Companion only sees the current track's sounit graph at a time. It does not see other tracks' graphs simultaneously.
  • Inspector panels do not hot-reload slider ranges when engine settings are changed via the Companion — close and reopen the inspector to see updated ranges.

Privacy

When using a cloud provider (Anthropic, OpenAI, Groq, OpenRouter), your conversation and the current graph state are sent to that provider's API servers. No data is collected by Kala itself. When using Ollama locally, nothing leaves your machine.

API keys are stored in your system's application settings (QSettings) and are not visible in plain text in the UI. They are sent only to the configured API endpoint.
